What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a person's weight in kilograms divided by the square of height in meters. BMI is an inexpensive and easy screening method for weight category—underweight, healthy weight, overweight, and obesity.

How to Calculate BMI

Formula: BMI = weight (kg) / height² (m²)

Example: 74 kg / (1.75m)² = 74 / 3.0625 = 24.2

Limitations of BMI

BMI is a useful tool but has limitations. It may overestimate body fat in athletes and people with a muscular build, and underestimate body fat in older adults and those who have lost muscle mass. For a more complete assessment, consider using our Body Fat Calculator alongside BMI.

BMI for Children and Teens

For children and teens, BMI is interpreted using age- and sex-specific percentiles from CDC growth charts. A child's weight status is determined by comparing their BMI to that of other children of the same age and sex.
